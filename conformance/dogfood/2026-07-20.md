# Source-Blind Dogfood — 2026-07-20

Contract: [`../expected-behavior.md`](../expected-behavior.md)

## Method

Seven bounded Claude Code Sonnet sessions covered greenfield iterations, brownfield audit, evidence reconciliation, and one semantic audit. Each received only one isolated fixture plus the packaged `graph-climbing` skill. They had no DACS, ISA, Beads, chat, or repository-history context. Runs were non-persistent and capped at USD 0.60 each; actual spend was not captured. Generated products stayed in temporary directories. This report preserves the durable findings without treating the transcripts as product evidence.

## Results

### Greenfield bootstrap

Result: structural pass; semantic findings retained.

- Created exactly one product authority: `SPEC.md`.
- Created no ledger, runtime, hook, dashboard, supervisor, or product code.
- Preserved the malformed-row and CSV-dialect ambiguities as `unknown` and kept their slice outside the active frontier.
- Derived one reachable well-formed-input slice and one unknown decision slice.
- Final structural check: 0 errors; one atomicity warning.
- Semantic inspection still found compound behavior in the trim/order and malformed-output claims, plus observable CLI/header assumptions described too casually as harmless. These remain evidence against claiming full bootstrap conformance.

The first attempt leaked a literal generation wrapper marker. The checker did not initially detect it. A regression rule now rejects exact leaked `<content>` or `<artifact>` wrapper lines. A later attempt invented RFC 4180 exclusions not present in the fixture. Another placed unknown claims inside an active slice; the hardened reachability check now rejects that graph. Bootstrap doctrine now requires unspecified material semantics to remain `unknown`, outside the frontier, and not be called harmless when they change observable behavior.

### Brownfield audit

Result: pass.

- Preserved the existing `PRODUCT.md`; created no competing `SPEC.md` or ledger.
- Made no file changes.
- Found a real evidence divergence: requirement `R-1` claimed `proven` using `help-v1.txt`, but the repository contained no source, test, commit, or fixture supporting that state.
- Reported the divergence and owner decision instead of mutating status.

### Evidence reconciliation

Result: pass.

- Mapped candidate evidence only to `C-2`.
- Changed `C-2` from `open` to `verified` and recorded its evidence.
- Changed only the owning slice `S-2` from `active` to `verified`.
- Preserved stable IDs and unresolved siblings.
- Derived an empty frontier and set `current_slice: none` after complete verification.
- Final structural check: 3/3 claims and 2/2 slices verified, 0 errors, 0 warnings.

## Anti-Cheat Probes

- Leaked generation marker: old artifact now fails with `unexpected_generation_marker`.
- Unsupported proof claim: semantic audit reports product/evidence divergence despite internally consistent prose.
- Unspecified product semantics: bootstrap records `unknown`; it does not manufacture a boundary.
- Compound claim/probe: the checker catches explicit conjunction patterns, while source-blind semantic inspection demonstrates why a mechanical pass remains internal consistency, never product proof.

## Disposition

The reference kit satisfies the brownfield-audit and reconciliation contract in this bounded sample. Greenfield bootstrap reached a structurally valid, authority-safe graph but did not yet satisfy every semantic-atomicity expectation. That limitation is recorded rather than converted into another review loop. Semantic atomicity remains a skill/audit judgment backed by claim probes, not a linter guarantee. The sample establishes neither universal productivity nor correctness. No additional governance control plane was introduced.

## Release-candidate review

A source-aware Claude Fable review assessed checker correctness, source-blind adoption, and public positioning after the initial dogfood. It judged the experimental v0.1 bundle releasable and found no evidence overstatement. Three accepted P3 findings were corrected: the external-adopter checker command, dropped claim tombstones incorrectly blocking live co-owned work, and non-dropped slices depending permanently on dropped slices. Regression coverage accompanies both checker changes.
